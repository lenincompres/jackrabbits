Jack Rabbits — Rules Written in Verse

Jack Rabbits is a modular fantasy board game whose entire instructional system is written in poetic meter.

Rather than separating rules from narrative or flavor text, Jack Rabbits presents its mechanics as formal verse designed to function as a complete, precise rulebook. The verses are not symbolic, metaphorical, or interpretive. They are written to be clear, direct, concise, and mechanically unambiguous, with rhyme and meter acting as an added structural constraint.

Two Languages, Two Meters

The rules exist in parallel poetic systems, each using a different classical structure:

  • Spanish rules are written in heroic verse, composed of hendecasyllabic lines (11 syllables) with three principal stresses per line, following traditional Spanish metrics used in didactic and epic writing.
  • English rules are written in trochaic tetrameter, consisting of four stressed–unstressed pairs per line, most often catalectic, ending on a final stressed syllable.

In both languages, syllable counts and stress patterns are intentionally controlled. The rules are authored to remain functionally equivalent, despite differences in rhythm and linguistic structure.

Clarity Under Constraint

The primary design goal of the rulebook is clarity and playability. All mechanics are expressed plainly, without allegory or poetic ambiguity.

Meter and rhyme are not used to embellish or obscure meaning, but to introduce an additional constraint—one that demands precision, economy of language, and careful structuring of instructions.

Why Verse?

Verse has long been used to preserve complex systems because meter aids memory, oral transmission, and consistency. Jack Rabbits applies this principle deliberately:

  • Rhythm supports recall during play
  • Fixed meter exposes inconsistencies quickly
  • Verse facilitates oral explanation and teaching
  • Constraint discourages redundancy and vagueness

Design as a Formal Experiment

Writing complete, playable rules in verse adds a design layer beyond balance and usability. Each mechanic must withstand:

  • Translation between languages
  • Translation between metrical systems
  • Playtesting without loss of precision

The poetic structure is not decoration; it is a design constraint that shapes how rules are conceived, refined, and tested.

A Contemporary Practice with Ancient Roots

Jack Rabbits draws inspiration from oral traditions, medieval didactic poetry, and constrained writing practices, while remaining a fully modern tabletop game.
